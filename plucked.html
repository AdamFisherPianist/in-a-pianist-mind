What Is a Plucked String?

In plucked string instruments, the string is pulled and released by the player or a mechanism. This sudden energy release creates a unique waveform, rich in harmonics but with a distinctive decay.

Common plucked instruments include the guitar, harp, lute, and harpsichord.

Sound Characteristics

Plucking introduces energy unevenly depending on pluck position and angle, which can emphasize or suppress certain harmonics. This gives plucked instruments a wide expressive range and tonal variation.

Compare to Struck Strings

While both use strings, plucked instruments let the performer shape the tone more directly. Struck strings (like the piano) create more consistent waveforms, while plucked strings vary based on technique.
