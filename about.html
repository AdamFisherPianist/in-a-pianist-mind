Who I Am

I’m a high school student from Morocco with a passion for both music and science. I’ve studied piano and solfège at the Conservatoire for over 8 years, earning my Premier Prix. I’ve also always loved math and physics — and this project reflects how I try to think like a scientist, but feel like an artist.

Academic & Musical Background

  • Conservatoire National de Musique (Morocco): 8 years of piano and 9 of solfège
  • Premier Prix in Solfège – awarded after national-level examinations in sight-reading, dication (hearing pitch) and music theory
  • 8th year of piano – classical training and theory-based interpretation
  • STEM Track – Sciences Mathématiques: Advanced physics, mathematics, and engineering sciences in high school

Other Projects & Passions

Outside of music and physics, I enjoy composing, audio editing, and exploring how digital tools like MIDI replicate real sound. I’ve also led a football team, love languages, and care about big-picture learning: how disciplines connect.

Why I Built This Website

This project started as a question: what’s really happening when you press a piano key? It evolved into a journey through harmonics, sound waves, and science. I created this site not only to learn more, but to show how music and science are never far apart — and how thinking across disciplines can help us hear the world more deeply.
